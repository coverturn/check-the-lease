# Round 9.8 — Build the Resources Hub

## What we're building

A new dedicated route at `/resources` that contains:

1. **National housing nonprofits** — orgs that work nationwide to help renters
2. **Free legal aid directory** — by state and territory (50 states + DC + 5 territories)
3. **Tenant union directory** — by major metro
4. **Emergency rental assistance** — federal + state programs
5. **Inclusion-focused services** — orgs serving immigrants, LGBTQ+, women, families, veterans, seniors

Plus surface a curated subset of this data inside the existing `/results/[id]` Resources tab so users get the relevant orgs based on their state.

## Part 1 — Research phase (Agent does this)

Agent: before writing copy or building the page, **research and verify**:

For each of the 50 states + DC + 5 territories (Puerto Rico, US Virgin Islands, Guam, American Samoa, Northern Mariana Islands), find:

- **At least 1 free legal aid organization** (e.g., Bay Area Legal Aid for CA, Legal Aid Society for NY)
- **At least 1 tenant rights organization** (e.g., Tenants Together for CA)
- **The state's official tenant rights page** (state AG or housing department)
- **The state's emergency rental assistance program URL**

For national-level, find:

- **National Low Income Housing Coalition** (nlihc.org)
- **National Housing Law Project** (nhlp.org)
- **LawHelp.org** — federal directory of free legal aid
- **HUD's tenant rights page**
- **Eviction Lab** (Princeton — research + data)
- **Lambda Legal** — LGBTQ+ legal support
- **National Network to End Domestic Violence** — DV-related housing
- **Catholic Charities** — emergency rental assistance often available
- **Salvation Army** — emergency rental assistance
- **211.org** — universal social services helpline
- **NeighborWorks America** — homeownership + counseling
- **American Bar Association free legal answers** (abafreelegalanswers.org)

For specific underserved groups, find at least 2 nationally recognized orgs each:
- **Immigrants** (e.g., National Immigration Law Center, CLINIC)
- **LGBTQ+** (e.g., Lambda Legal, NCLR)
- **Women / Domestic violence survivors** (e.g., NNEDV, RAINN)
- **Veterans** (e.g., VA tenant programs, US Vets)
- **Seniors** (e.g., AARP, NCOA)
- **Disabled renters** (e.g., Disability Rights Education & Defense Fund)
- **Families with children** (e.g., Children's Defense Fund housing programs)

**Verify each URL is live before adding it.** Dead links destroy the credibility this section is supposed to build.

## Part 3 — Build `/resources` page

Create a new route at `/resources`. Same nav and footer as the homepage.

### Page structure

**Section 1 — Hero**

- Eyebrow: `RESOURCES`
- H1: *"Help, when the lease isn't enough."*
- Sub: *"Free legal aid, tenant rights organizations, and housing support — by state and nationally. Curated, verified, and updated. We don't take referral fees."*

**Section 2 — National housing organizations**

- Eyebrow: `NATIONAL`
- H2: *"Help that works anywhere in the US."*
- Body: short paragraph explaining these orgs serve every state
- 6-8 cards in a 2-col grid (1-col mobile), each with: name, description, URL link

**Section 3 — Find help by state**

- Eyebrow: `BY STATE`
- H2: *"Click your state to see what's available."*
- Body: short paragraph
- A clean state picker — could reuse the same interactive US map component from the homepage, OR a simple dropdown + filtered list below
- When a state is selected, show:
  - The state's official tenant rights page (link)
  - The state's emergency rental assistance program (link)
  - Legal aid organizations (cards)
  - Tenant unions (cards)

**Section 4 — Help for specific situations**

- Eyebrow: `SPECIFIC SITUATIONS`
- H2: *"If you're facing a particular challenge."*
- Body: introduces the filter approach
- Filter pills: All · Immigrants · LGBTQ+ · Women & DV survivors · Veterans · Seniors · Disabled · Families
- Below: cards filtered by the selected pill, showing relevant national orgs

**Section 5 — Inclusion statement**

- Eyebrow: `WHY THIS EXISTS`
- H2 (Fraunces italic, ~32px): *"The renters who need help most are the ones least likely to find it."*
- Body:
  > *"Single parents, immigrants, low-income families, seniors on fixed income, LGBTQ+ renters facing discrimination, domestic violence survivors needing emergency housing, veterans transitioning out of service. The people who get burned hardest by bad leases are the people least likely to have a lawyer's number on speed dial. This page exists to put one there."*

**Section 6 — Disclaimer**

- Standard disclaimer block:
  > *"Check the Lease provides information about these organizations. We're not affiliated with any of them. We don't take referral fees. We do verify every link before listing it. If you find a broken or outdated entry, email hello@checkthelease.com and we'll fix it."*

5. **It's the move that takes Check the Lease from "hackathon project" to "credible nonprofit-adjacent service."** Big credibility lift.
