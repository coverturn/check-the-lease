# Part 1 — The non-negotiable copywriting rules

These are the locked principles. Every edit you make must respect every rule.

## Rule 1 — Forward-looking framing (LOCKED)

**Never imply the reader is missing something. Always describe what we deliver.**

| ❌ Hidden criticism | ✅ Forward-looking |
|---|---|
| "Your lease is full of risks" | "Things to be aware of in your lease" |
| "You don't know what you're signing" | "We help you know exactly what you're signing" |
| "Your landlord is taking advantage of you" | "Some clauses favour landlords more than renters" |
| "You need a lawyer" | "We do the lawyer-level read for you" |

**The test:** Read each sentence. Does it point out something the reader DOESN'T have? If yes, rewrite. Does it describe something we deliver? If yes, ship it.

## Rule 2 — Customer-as-hero (StoryBrand framework)

The customer is the hero of the story. Check the Lease is the guide. Never position the product as the star.

| ❌ Product-as-hero | ✅ Customer-as-hero |
|---|---|
| "Check the Lease analyses every clause" | "Read every clause of your lease in plain English" |
| "Our AI catches risky language" | "Catch the clauses that could cost you" |
| "We have the most comprehensive state coverage" | "Know what your state actually requires" |

## Rule 3 — Plain English at Grade 6 reading level

Test every sentence against the Hemingway app standard:
- Sentence length: 8–14 words average
- Avoid passive voice
- Avoid adverbs that don't add meaning ("really", "very", "actually")
- Avoid legal / SaaS jargon

| ❌ Jargon | ✅ Plain |
|---|---|
| "statute" | "law" |
| "habitability" | "living conditions" |
| "tenant protection statutes" | "tenant rules" |
| "implied warranty" | "the protection you're entitled to" → rewrite to remove "entitled to" → "the protection your state guarantees" |
| "unenforceable" | "won't hold up in court" |
| "right of entry" | "when the landlord can come in" |
| "indemnification clause" | "who pays if something goes wrong" |
| "non-refundable deposit" | "deposit you can't get back" |
| "automatically" | "on its own" (in conversational context) |

## Rule 4 — Banned phrases (compliance-critical)

These phrases must appear NOWHERE in user-facing copy:

- "you should"
- "you must"
- "this is illegal"
- "I recommend"
- "sue your landlord"
- "you are entitled to"
- "you have the right to" (passive — instead use "California law gives you the right to")

Allowed alternatives:

| Banned | Replace with |
|---|---|
| "you should ask" | "consider asking" |
| "you must check" | "worth checking" |
| "this is illegal" | "this may be unenforceable" or "courts have ruled this unenforceable" |
| "I recommend" | "consider" or "worth knowing" |
| "you are entitled to" | "California law gives you the right to" or "your state requires" |

## Rule 5 — Three-claim limit per section (Cialdini overload threshold)

Beyond 3 positive claims in any section, perception of manipulation increases. Audit every section. If there are more than 3 specific claims being made, cut to 3.

## Rule 6 — Audience balance (dual-audience pattern)

This product serves **renters AND landlords equally**. Every general section must speak to both, OR explicitly label which audience it addresses.

❌ Don't write copy that paints landlords as villains. The landlord using Check the Lease is on your side — they want a fair, compliant lease too.

The single root cause of most copy issues on this site: **the page evolved with renter-first writing, then landlord copy was added later without refactoring the early sections.** Apply the dual-audience pattern from the existing "Who It's For" section to every other general section.

## Rule 7 — American spellings (US product, US audience)

Find and replace globally:

|---|---|
| programmes | programs |
| programme | program |
| organisations | organizations |
| organisation | organization |
| optimised | optimized |
| analysed | analyzed |
| analyser | analyzer |
| behaviour | behavior |
| colour | color |

| favourite | favorite |

| realise | realize |
| recognised | recognized |
| prioritised | prioritized |
| summarised | summarized |
| tokenised | tokenized |
| neighbour | neighbor |
| labour | labor |
| utilised | utilized |

Search every `.tsx`, `.ts`, `.md`, `.json`, `.html`, `.css`, `.txt` file in the codebase.

## Rule 8 — CTA button copy (specific rules)

- Maximum 2–5 words
- First-person ("My" not "Your") — converts 124% better per industry data
- Action verb first
- Outcome-focused (what they GET, not what they DO)

Locked CTAs across the site:
- Primary: **"Read my lease →"**
- Secondary: **"Try a sample lease →"**
- "Analyse another lease →" → **"Read another lease →"** (if anywhere)

## Rule 9 — Specificity over generic

Vague claims trigger BS detectors. Specific claims build trust.

| ❌ Vague | ✅ Specific |
|---|---|
| "We help you understand your lease" | "Plain-English read with citations to your state's actual law" |
| "Many leases have problems" | "40% of US leases contain unenforceable clauses" |
| "Save money on your deposit" | "Spot the clauses that could cost you your deposit" |
| "Used by many renters" | "45 million US renters could use this" |

## Section 01 — The Problem (CRITICAL — currently renter-only)

**Why:** Inclusive — neither side is the villain. The system is the villain. Both renters and landlords have the same root problem.

### Body paragraph

**Find** (exact match):

Landlords inherit templates from property managers — full of clauses courts have already voided. Both sides end up with paperwork that doesn't reflect what they actually agreed to."*

**Why:** Acknowledges both sides. Shorter sentences (mobile-friendly per skill rules). Removes adversarial framing. Adds specificity (35 pages, courts have voided).

**Find** (exact match):

**Replace with**:
> *"A fair lease should protect both signatures. Most don't."*

**Why:** Inclusive. Punchy two-sentence rhythm.
