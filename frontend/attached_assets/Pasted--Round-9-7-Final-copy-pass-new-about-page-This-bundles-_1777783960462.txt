### Page structure

**Layout:** same nav and footer as the homepage. Single-column max-width 720px container, padding 64px 28px desktop / 48px 20px mobile.

### Section 1 — About hero

**Eyebrow:** `ABOUT`

**H1 (Fraunces 500, ~64px desktop, clamp scaled):**
> A free lease reader for everyone who signs.

**Sub (Inter 16px, ink-muted, max-width 540px):**

### Section 2 — The story

**Eyebrow:** `THE STORY`

**H2:** *"How Check the Lease started."*

**Body (paragraphs, Inter 15px, ink, line-height 1.65):**

> *"In May 2026, Replit ran a 24-hour buildathon to celebrate its 10th anniversary. I'd been thinking about lease analysis for a while — every renter friend I have has at some point signed something they didn't understand and then got burned by it later. Deposits not returned. Surprise fees. Landlords who thought they could enter whenever.*
>
> *I built Check the Lease in those 24 hours. Cream backgrounds, hand-drawn illustrations, real Claude API analysis against a curated database of California tenant law. One person, one weekend, the prototype that became this site.*
>
> *What started as a hackathon submission stayed live because the problem isn't going away. 45 million US renters. 40% of leases contain unenforceable clauses. Most renters don't have a lawyer. Most landlords inherit templates they've never read.*
>
> *Check the Lease exists to put a plain-English layer between the contract and the people signing it. Free, cited to actual law, balanced for both sides. Because reading a lease shouldn't require a law degree."*

### Section 3 — What I believe

**Eyebrow:** `WHAT I BELIEVE`

**H2:** *"The principles."*

**4 principle cards** (2x2 grid desktop, 1-col mobile, bone bg + 1.5px border + 16px radius + 24px padding):

   **H3:** *"Every lease deserves a fair read."*
   **Body:** *"Whether the signature is yours or your tenant's. The lease is a contract between two people — both should understand it."*

2. **Eyebrow:** *"Principle 02"*
   **H3:** *"Plain English over legalese."*
   **Body:** *"If a clause needs a law degree to parse, it's hiding something. Every flag I surface is written at a sixth-grade reading level."*

3. **Eyebrow:** *"Principle 03"*
   **H3:** *"Free, for everyone."*
   **Body:** *"The renters who get burned hardest by bad leases are the ones least likely to afford a lawyer. A free product helps them most. And free for landlords too — fair lease drafts protect both sides."*

4. **Eyebrow:** *"Principle 04"*
   **H3:** *"Cited to actual law."*
   **Body:** *"Every claim links to the specific statute. Not "AI thinks this might be illegal" — "California Civil Code section 1950.5 caps your deposit at two months." Verifiable, every time."*
